## GEO: Write for AI Citations

AI search engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Google AI Overviews) cite web content in their answers. Structure your copy so it gets cited:

### Citable Passages
- **Ideal length**: 134-167 words per citable block, self-contained, fact-rich
- **Lead paragraphs**: Must directly answer the page's core question. No warm-up intros
- **Structure**: Each section should work as a standalone excerpt. If someone reads just that paragraph, they should understand the point

### Heading Patterns
- Use Q&A heading patterns that match how people ask AI: `## What is X?`, `## How does X work?`, `## X vs Y: Which is better?`
- These map directly to AI search queries and increase citation probability

### Facts Over Filler
- Include specific numbers, dates, percentages, and comparisons. AI engines strongly prefer citing passages with concrete data
- Bad: "We significantly improved performance"
- Good: "Response times dropped from 2.3s to 180ms after the migration"

### Attribution and E-E-A-T
- Include author name and credentials where appropriate
- Reference sources, dates, and methodology
- Experience signals: "In our testing...", "After analyzing 500 deployments..."
- These signals help AI engines evaluate content trustworthiness for citation
